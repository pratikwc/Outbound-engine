# Industry Classification Map

The research agent uses this file to classify a prospect's company into the correct industry bucket. Get this right — it determines which social proof gets used in their email.

---

## How to Classify

**Step 1:** Check the company's LinkedIn industry field first.
**Step 2:** If LinkedIn is ambiguous or wrong, check: what kind of jobs does this company hire for? That tells you the real bucket.
**Step 3:** Match to one of the buckets below.
**Step 4:** Output the bucket name in the research brief so the outreach agent can pull the right case study from social_proof.md.

## Industry Buckets

---

### Bucket 1 — Horeca (Hotel, Restaurant, Cafe)
**LinkedIn nodes:** Hospitality, Food and Beverage Services, Restaurants
**Who goes here:** Hotels, restaurant chains, cafe chains, entertainment venues with F&B, catering companies
**What they hire for:** Kitchen staff, chefs, waiters, reception, hotel operations, housekeeping, service staff
**WC clients in this bucket:** Alabbar Enterprises, Chedi Hospitality, Ethos
**Note:** Alabbar is the F&B/operations arm of a broader group — maps to this bucket. Chedi is a hotel chain — maps here too.

### Bucket 2 — Retail / Wholesale / Distribution
**LinkedIn nodes:** Retail, Wholesale, Wholesale Import and Export
**Who goes here:** Companies with physical stores, retail outlets, distribution networks, wholesale import/export operations

**WC clients in this bucket:** SARA Group, Bidfood Middle East, Madi International, WOLFI'S, The Luxury Closet, The Petshop, Chemist Warehouse UAE
**Note:** Bidfood is an F&B distributor — maps here, not Horeca. SARA Group and Madi are retail/wholesale holding groups — not hospitality. Daikan: verify on LinkedIn before placing (may be Wholesale or Horeca).

## If You Can't Classify

1. Check the company's careers page — what kind of jobs are they posting?
2. Check their LinkedIn "About" section and description
3. If still unclear, classify as "Other / Mixed" and flag in research brief
4. Never guess and assign confidently — a wrong industry = wrong social proof = lower response rate
