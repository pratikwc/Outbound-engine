# Avatar Cheat Sheet — HR Personas

Three personas. One DM, two Champions. Never mix in the same campaign sequence.

Apollo titles → persona mapping at the bottom.

---

## Persona A — Decision Maker (CEO / CHRO)
- **Titles**: CEO, Owner, Founder, CHRO, Chief People Officer, Chief Human Resources Officer
- **Apollo list**: Decision Maker (CXO/Director)
- **Company size**: 100–1,000 employees (at <300 they're often DM + HR lead combined)
- **Day-to-day**: P&L, headcount planning, board reporting, scaling teams
- **KPIs they're measured on**: Cost-per-hire, time-to-fill, revenue per headcount, agency spend
- **Biggest pain right now**: Hiring is slow and expensive — agencies eat margin, process is opaque
- **What they fear**: Missing growth targets because they can't hire fast enough
- **What they secretly want**: Remove agency dependency entirely, build a hiring machine that runs itself
- **Email angle**: Business outcome and ROI — "cut cost-per-hire, stop paying 12% to agencies"
- **Resonating stats**: 44 days → 2 days time-to-hire (Alabbar Enterprises) · agencies charge 8–15% annual CTC · 91 companies in UAE/KSA use Whitecarrot
- **Objection to expect**: "We have a recruiter handling this" / "Not a priority right now"
- **CTA that works**: "Worth a 15-min call?" or "Mind if I show you how [similar company] did it?"

---

## Persona B — TA Champion (Talent Acquisition)
- **Titles**: Talent Acquisition Manager, Talent Acquisition Director, Head of Talent Acquisition, Recruitment Manager, Recruitment Team Leader, Talent Lead
- **Apollo list**: HR Influencer/Champion
- **Company size**: 200–800 employees (needs enough hiring volume to feel the pain daily)
- **Day-to-day**: Owns the full recruiting funnel — sourcing, screening, coordinating interviews, managing JDs
- **KPIs they're measured on**: Time-to-fill, offers made, candidate quality, hiring manager satisfaction
- **Biggest pain right now**: Screening CVs manually for every role — 18 hours per role on average
- **What they fear**: Good candidates dropping off because the process moves too slowly
- **What they secretly want**: A tool that makes them look organised and efficient to hiring managers and leadership
- **Email angle**: Day-to-day pain — "18 hours screening CVs per role, candidates accepting other offers while you wait"
- **Resonating stats**: Screening 18 hrs → minutes · 850K+ candidates in system · AI ranks top 20 CVs per role
- **Objection to expect**: "We already use [tool]" / "I need to check with my manager"
- **CTA that works**: "Happy to show you what the screening looks like in 15 mins" or "Can I send you a quick demo?"

---

## Persona C — HR Champion (HR Manager / Director)
- **Titles**: HR Manager, HR Director, VP People, Head of HR, Human Resources Manager, Human Resources Director
- **Apollo list**: HR Influencer/Champion
- **Company size**: 100–500 employees (often a team of 1–3, wearing multiple hats)
- **Day-to-day**: Recruiting, compliance, onboarding, performance reviews — all at once
- **KPIs they're measured on**: Time-to-hire, offer acceptance rate, headcount plan delivery
- **Biggest pain right now**: No single view of all open roles and candidates — everything lives in email, WhatsApp, spreadsheets
- **What they fear**: Losing a good candidate because the process was too slow or disorganised
- **What they secretly want**: A system that runs itself so they can focus on strategic HR work, not admin
- **Email angle**: Process and visibility — "no centralised pipeline means candidates fall through the cracks"
- **Resonating stats**: One platform from headcount planning to onboarding · Beehive case study (BambooHR → Whitecarrot, HMs fill junior roles without assigning a recruiter)
- **Objection to expect**: "We're managing fine with what we have" / "Not the right time"
- **CTA that works**: "Worth a quick look?" or "Can I show you how teams your size are running this?"
