# Objection Handling

Common objections + approved responses. Use these as the base — personalise to the specific reply.

---

## "We already use [ATS / tool]"

**Response**:
{{first_name}}, makes sense. A lot of teams we work with were using [tool] before — usually the issue was adoption, not the tool itself.

If your team is actually using it and it's working, no point switching. But if hiring managers still default to email, happy to show you what we do differently in 10 mins.

---

## "Not the right time"

**Response**:
No problem, {{first_name}}. When would be a better time? Happy to follow up in [X weeks/months] if hiring picks back up then.

*(If they say Q3/Q4, log in CRM and set reminder.)*

---

## "Who are you?" / "How did you find me?"

**Response**:
Fair question — I'm Pratik from Whitecarrot, an ATS built for growing teams in the region. Found you via [LinkedIn / Apollo / referral].

Saw {{company_name}} has been hiring and thought it might be relevant. If not, happy to leave you alone — just say the word.

---

## "Send more info"

**Response**:
Sure — here's a quick overview: [link to one-pager or website]

The faster path is usually a 10-min call where I can show you what it looks like for a team your size. Open to that?

---

## "Budget is frozen"

**Response**:
Totally understand. Most teams we work with make the switch when they're in a hiring push and the cost of a bad process becomes obvious.

If the timing changes, I'm easy to find. Good luck with the current quarter.

---

## Rules
- Never be defensive or pushy after an objection
- Always leave the door open — don't burn the lead
- If unsure, escalate to Pratik (see handover_rules.md)
